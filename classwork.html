What i Learned

Reflection From the Day 1 Topic to now (with css)

From the start of learning HCI, HTML, and CSS, I honestly thought making a website would be super easy like just typing codes and everything will just work. But when we started, I realized it’s not that simple at all.

HTML Basics

At first, HTML was kind of confusing for me because of all the tags like head, body, div, section, and more. I didn’t really understand it in the beginning, but later on I realized HTML is like the structure or skeleton of the website. It’s where you put all the contents like text, images, and headings. Once I got used to it, I started to understand how important every tag is.

CSS Text Properties

Then we moved to CSS, and that’s where things got more interesting. CSS is the one that makes the website look good and presentable. It’s where you add colors, fonts, spacing, and layout. At first, I struggled a lot with things like margins, padding, and positioning because sometimes my design wouldn’t look right or things were out of place. But as I kept practicing, I slowly learned how it works and how small changes can affect the whole design.

HTML Layouts

Through HCI, I also learned that making a website is not just about coding, but also about making it user-friendly. It should be easy to read, easy to navigate, and not confusing for the users. My journey from HTML to CSS felt like building something step by step. I still have a lot to learn, but now I understand that web design is a mix of patience, creativity, and basic logic.
